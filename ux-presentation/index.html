The Psychology of Choice in Design

The Definition

Hick's Law (or the Hick-Hyman Law) describes the time it takes for a person to make a decision as a result of the possible choices they have.

"The time it takes to make a decision increases with the number and complexity of choices."

Real-world Example

Think about the Netflix Homepage vs. an old TV Remote.

Netflix

Categorized rows (Top 10, Trending, Action). You aren't shown 5,000 movies at once. You browse small chunks. This reduces cognitive load.

The Old TV Remote

40+ buttons of equal size. Finding "Input" took seconds because you had to scan every single option. Bad application of Hick's Law.

Design Application

How do we use this in business software?

  • Progressive Disclosure: Show advanced options only when needed.
  • Categorization: Group menu items meaningfully (e.g., "File", "Edit").
  • Highlighting: Make the primary call-to-action (CTA) distinct.

Key Takeaway

Simplicity is not about removing features.

It's about organizing them to reduce decision time.

Simplify the path to value.
